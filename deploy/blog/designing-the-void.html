Designing the Void

How extremely minimal modern interiors use negative space to elevate human experience.

An empty gallery room glowing with muted overcast light is not simply 'empty'. It is a deliberate volume of curated negative space. By pulling back, we allow the occupant's mind to expand into the architecture. Concrete flooring and pale ash walls provide an acoustic and visual resting place, ensuring that the void itself becomes the focal point. It is in these silent gaps that true architectural resonance occurs.
